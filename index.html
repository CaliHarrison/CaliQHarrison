.GET TO KNOW ME.

HELLO!

I want to personally welcome you to my portfolio.

I love to do graphic design, take, and edit photos, and film and edit videos.

Ever since I was little, I have loved four things: taking pictures, creating videos, watching and playing sports, and putting it all together through graphic design. But it wasn’t until I suffered a severe concussion in college while snowboarding that I knew I wanted to pursue these passions as a full-time career.

As a child, I begged my parents for a camera. I finally broke them and received my first camera - a Red Nikon digital camera with a video recording option. I took pictures of everything - the cats, the dogs, my bedroom, my dolls, even an empty chair! I took it to a Taylor Swift concert and that’s when I realized I loved videography as well.

Throughout my childhood, I was the one coming up with different ideas for filming and photoshoots among my neighborhood friends. It also helped that my best friend’s brother was older than us and knew a little more about directing than we did, but I was always filming when I wasn’t in the scenes. A few years later, I received a video camera and started recording all my road trips and adventures with my family and friends.

When I hit junior high, my childhood fun paid off with a position on the yearbook staff, where I captured all sorts of content. I also helped with our student-run media productions by filming and editing video. Fast-forward to my senior year in high school when I had to film and edit a video for my Spanish class. It ran on youtube and was so catchy and entertaining!

Now, to sports. I have loved attending sporting events all my life, whether it was football, basketball, soccer, volleyball, or intramural sports. From trying almost every sport when I was little (Tennis anyone? How about ice skating? Soccer? Horseback riding? Softball?) to being involved with my high school sports teams (Go Upland!), sports have brought such joy to my life.

During high school, I participated in color guard (leaping, marching, twirling flags) and ran hurdles and relays on the track and field team. During track season, I hurt my foot and had to visit the athletic trainers. It was amazing! I loved the camaraderie and the medical aspect. So, I joined my school’s highly regarded athletic training program during my last two years of high school, working with players in every sport.

I thought that was my destiny. I headed off to college as a Pre-Athletic training major. But fate intervened midway through my freshman year. During a snowboarding class, a patch of ice and a tumble down the mountain left me with such a severe concussion that I missed three weeks of school. When I returned, I was sitting in one of my athletic training classes and had an epiphany: I didn’t want to be a trainer. It was too rigid, too structured. I realized it wasn’t the training itself I liked, but the camaraderie with the various teams. And since I had always been a creative person, that’s what was missing from training.

So, upon the recommendation of a sorority sister, I applied for an internship with the sports marketing department at my university. That’s when I knew what I wanted to do with the rest of my life for my career. I learned so many valuable skills: photoshop, action-shot photography, how to create an eye-catching social media account. Getting to combine all the things I loved was a dream come true!

In my free time, I continue to film my travels and make fun, short clips of my life. Now, with the world of Snapchat, Instagram, Tiktok, Twitter, Youtube, and Facebook, I get to practice all sorts of things and post for the world to see!
